学术成果 Publications

打破人类主导地位：探究学习者对生成式人工智能与人类导师学习反馈的偏好 Breaking human dominance: Investigating learners' preferences for learning feedback from generative AI and human tutors 第三作者 Third Author

作者1, 作者2, 李子健, 等

Author 1, Author 2, Zijian Li, et al.

British Journal of Educational Technology (BJET), 2024 (已接收) British Journal of Educational Technology (BJET), 2024 (Accepted)

在教育情境中理解学习者的偏好，对于优化学习成效与学习体验至关重要。随着人工智能（AI）日益融入教育实践，有必要把握学习者在 AI 与人类导师之间的偏好差异，以更有效地支持其学习。尽管 AI 在教育中的潜力不断增强，但“算法厌恶”（algorithm aversion）现象——即相较于算法解决方案更倾向于信任人类决策的倾向——仍有待进一步探究。为考察这一问题，我们开展了一项实验，招募 114 名大学生，在其分别接触四种条件之一之前与之后，测量他们对不同反馈来源的偏好。这四种条件包括：无反馈、人类导师反馈、通过自由对话式用户界面提供的 ChatGPT 反馈，以及通过结构化界面提供的 AI 写作分析工具反馈。 研究结果显示，学习者在任务前对人类导师具有显著的初始偏好。然而，任务后分析揭示了一个重要的细微差别：总体上对人类导师的偏好仍然持续，但学习者对 ChatGPT 的自由对话式界面（ChatGPT 4.0）的偏好有所提升；相对地，结构化的 AI 界面（AI 写作分析工具）则进一步强化了学习者对人类导师的偏好。上述发现在理论与实践层面均具有贡献：一方面，将算法厌恶理论拓展至教育情境；另一方面，表明恰当的交互设计能够缓解此类厌恶。自由对话式界面的成功提示，克服算法厌恶可能更依赖于营造自然、灵活的交互体验，而非仅仅诉诸技术层面的优化。与此同时，我们也必须关注：对 AI 工具（尤其是具有更强吸引力的界面者）偏好的提升，可能引发学习者对技术支持的过度依赖与元认知惰性（metacognitive laziness），从而凸显在提供技术支持与培养独立学习能力之间保持平衡的重要性。

Understanding learners' preferences in educational settings is crucial for optimizing learning outcomes and experience. As artificial intelligence (AI) becomes increasingly integrated into educational contexts, it is crucial to understand learners' preferences between AI and human tutors to support their learning. While AI demonstrates growing potential in education, the phenomenon of algorithm aversion, which is a tendency to favour human decision making over algorithmic solutions, requires further investigation. To explore this issue, an experiment involving 114 university students was conducted to measure learners' preferences for different feedback sources before and after exposure to one of four conditions: no feedback, human tutor feedback, ChatGPT feedback through a free-dialogue user interface, and AI-powered writing analytics tool feedback through a structured interface. Our results revealed a strong initial preference for human tutors. However, the post-task analysis showed an important nuance. While the general preference for human tutors persisted, learners' preference towards the free-dialogue interface (ChatGPT 4.0) of ChatGPT increased, whereas the structured AI interface (AI-powered writing analytics tool) reinforced the preference for human tutors. These findings offer theoretical and practical contributions by extending algorithm aversion theory to educational contexts and demonstrating that appropriate interaction design can mitigate this aversion. The success of free-dialogue interfaces suggests that overcoming algorithm aversion may depend more on creating natural, flexible interaction experiences than purely technical optimization. However, we must also consider that increased preference for AI tools, particularly those with more engaging interfaces, may potentially lead to over-reliance and metacognitive laziness among learners, highlighting the importance of balancing technological support with the development of independent learning skills.

生成式人工智能如何影响元认知与自我调节学习：促进还是抑制？ How GenAI Affects Metacognition and Self-Regulated Learning: Enhancement or Inhibition? 第一作者 First Author

李子健, 范逸洲

Zijian Li, Yizhou Fan

Routledge 图书章节, 2024 (已接收) In: Routledge Book Chapter, 2024 (Accepted)

本章探讨生成式人工智能（GenAI）对自我调节学习（self-regulated learning, SRL）中元认知的影响，重点关注 ChatGPT 等 GenAI 工具如何作用于 SRL 三个阶段中的元认知技能发展。通过回顾近期的实证研究（包括我们自身的研究），我们分析了 GenAI 对关键元认知要素的影响，例如计划、监控与评价。研究发现表明，GenAI 具有提升学生元认知能力的潜力，使其成为更高效且更具自主性的学习者。 但本章也强调了与“元认知惰性”相关的风险：学生可能对 AI 工具产生过度依赖，从而降低认知投入。尽管 GenAI 可能支持积极的学习结果，这些风险仍凸显了采取策略以减轻其负面影响的必要性。最后，本章呼吁进一步研究 GenAI 所诱发的元认知惰性，并开发相应干预措施，以确保 GenAI 在 SRL 框架下促进更深层的元认知投入。

This chapter explores the impact of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) on metacognition in self-regulated learning (SRL), particularly focusing on how GenAI tools like ChatGPT influence the development of metacognitive skills across the three phases of SRL. Through a review of recent empirical studies, including our own research, we analyze the effects of GenAI on critical metacognitive components, such as planning, monitoring and evaluation. The findings indicate that GenAI has the potential to enhance students’ metacognitive capabilities, empowering them to become more effective and autonomous learners. However, the chapter also highlights the risks associated with metacognitive laziness, where students may over-rely on AI tools, resulting in diminished cognitive engagement. While GenAI can support positive learning outcomes, these risks underscore the need for strategies to mitigate its negative effects. The chapter concludes by advocating for further research into metacognitive laziness induced by GenAI and the development of interventions to ensure that GenAI fosters deeper metacognitive engagement within SRL frameworks.

当大语言模型在演绎式编码中力有未逮：模型对比与人机协作工作流设计 When LLMs fall short in Deductive Coding: Model Comparison and Human AI Collaboration Workflow Design 第一作者 First Author

李子健, 等

Zijian Li, et al.

arXiv预印本, 2024 arXiv Preprint, 2024

随着生成式人工智能推动教育领域对话式数据的增长，自动化编码作为学习分析提升效率的一条有前景的路径日益受到关注。这一激增也凸显出理解学生—AI 交互细微差别的必要性，尤其是那些发生频率较低却至关重要的交互类型。然而，由于数据分布不均衡，自动化编码可能难以捕捉这些低频代码；与此同时，人工编码仍然耗时且劳动密集。 本研究考察了大语言模型（LLMs）在演绎式、理论驱动编码中逼近或替代人工编码的潜力，并进一步探讨人机协作如何支持此类编码任务的规模化开展。我们在两个数据集上比较了小型 Transformer 分类器（如 BERT）与大语言模型的编码表现，尤其关注对话代码中不均衡的“头—尾”分布。结果表明，LLMs 并未优于基于 BERT 的模型，并且在演绎式编码任务中呈现出系统性的错误与偏差。我们据此设计并评估了一种人机协作工作流，在保持编码信度的同时提升了编码效率。总体而言，本研究既揭示了 LLMs 的局限——尤其是在语义相似性判断与理论性阐释方面的困难——也凸显了人类判断的不可或缺性，同时展示了用于编码任务的人机协作工作流在实践层面的应用潜力。

With generative artificial intelligence driving the growth of dialogic data in education, automated coding is a promising direction for learning analytics to improve efficiency. This surge highlights the need to understand the nuances of student-AI interactions, especially those rare yet crucial. However, automated coding may struggle to capture these rare codes due to imbalanced data, while human coding remains time-consuming and labour-intensive. The current study examined the potential of large language models (LLMs) to approximate or replace humans in deductive, theory-driven coding, while also exploring how human-AI collaboration might support such coding tasks at scale. We compared the coding performance of small transformer classifiers (e.g., BERT) and LLMs in two datasets, with particular attention to imbalanced head-tail distributions in dialogue codes. Our results showed that LLMs did not outperform BERT-based models and exhibited systematic errors and biases in deductive coding tasks. We designed and evaluated a human-AI collaborative workflow that improved coding efficiency while maintaining coding reliability. Our findings reveal both the limitations of LLMs -- especially their difficulties with semantic similarity and theoretical interpretations and the indispensable role of human judgment -- while demonstrating the practical promise of human-AI collaborative workflows for coding.
